# Comparative Simulation Results — Phase 0 Format Testing

**Status:** [SIMULATED] Tier 4 evidence. Hypotheses only. Needs real-founder validation.

**Setup:** 3 artifact variants × 2 idea archetypes × 4 personas = 24 evaluations.

---

## Variants Tested

| Variant | Structure | Key Difference |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| **A: "The Autopsy"** | Assumption verdicts + uncomfortable truth + next steps | Minimal, focused, no decision call |
| **B: "The Verdict"** | PROCEED/PIVOT/PAUSE up top + confidence + respondent transparency + difficulty-rated next steps | Decision-forward, transparent |
| **C: "The Dossier"** | A + competitive landscape + business model assessment + risk matrix | Comprehensive, analytical |

## Personas Used

1. Skeptical second-time founder (30, failed before, cautious)
2. First-time ambitious founder (22, excited, emotionally attached)
3. Experienced PM/operator (32, analytical, hard to impress)
4. Adversarial breaker (stress-tests credibility and actionability)

## Idea Archetypes

1. **Onboardly** — B2B SaaS (onboarding flow builder)
2. **SkillSwap** — Consumer marketplace (peer skill exchange)

## Comparative Results

### Which variant performed best overall?

**Variant B ("The Verdict") performed best across personas and ideas.**

It consistently scored highest on:
- Decision-changing potential (the PROCEED/PIVOT/PAUSE call forced clarity)
- Trust (respondent transparency section reduced skepticism)
- Actionability (difficulty/risk ratings on next steps grounded the advice)

Variant C was second — the competitive landscape and unit economics were valued by PMs and skeptical founders but created length that reduced impact for first-timers and drew fire from the adversarial breaker for being potentially stale or AI-generated.

Variant A was weakest — clean and impactful but missing the decision structure and transparency that B provides.

### Which most often felt decision-changing?

**Variant B.** The top-line PROCEED/PIVOT/PAUSE call was cited by 6 of 8 non-breaker personas as "the thing that would actually change what I do." First-time founders specifically said they needed the meta-decision made for them, not just evidence to interpret.

Variant A's uncomfortable truth section was equally powerful emotionally, but without the explicit decision call, founders weren't sure what to DO with it.

### Which most often triggered trust?

**Variant B** — because of the respondent transparency section. The skeptical founder and PM persona both cited it as the reason they'd take the findings more seriously. Even though the transparency revealed limitations (n=20, self-selected), being honest about those limitations increased trust rather than decreased it.

Variant A had no transparency → trust relied entirely on quote quality.
Variant C had methodology gaps in the competitive/business sections that actively undermined trust.

### Which most often felt fake-smart or weak?

**Variant C's competitive landscape and risk matrix** were the most consistently criticized sections across all personas. Specific complaints:
- Competitive tables felt "surface-level" / "what I could do in 10 minutes of Googling"
- No citations or dates on competitor data → suspected AI-generated from training data
- Risk matrix severity/likelihood ratings were "vibes wearing a lab coat"
- Business model math used unsourced assumptions ("typical $200-500 CAC")

Variant A's next steps were also criticized as "generic marketplace playbook" by skeptical founders and breakers.

### Which sections consistently worked across ALL variants?

1. **Assumption verdicts with quotes** — Universally praised. The "extract assumptions → test individually → verdict" structure was cited as the core differentiator from ChatGPT by every non-breaker persona.
2. **The uncomfortable truth** — Consistently the most emotionally impactful section. All personas described it as "the thing that hit hardest."
3. **Supply/demand quantification** (for marketplace ideas) — Concrete numbers + behavioral implications were valued by all experience levels.

### Which sections consistently failed across variants?

1. **Generic next steps** — "Start with one community," "solve supply first" criticized as YC lecture material, not data-derived insight. Repeated across all variants.
2. **Competitive landscape** (Variant C) — Surface-level, unsourced, potentially stale. Damaged credibility more than it helped.
3. **Risk matrices** (Variant C) — Severity/likelihood ratings with no methodology. "Vibes wearing a lab coat."
4. **Confidence labels without methodology** — "HIGH" / "MEDIUM" confidence on n=20 drew fire from PMs and breakers across all variants.

### Which weaknesses are FORMAT problems vs EVIDENCE problems?

| Weakness | Format or Evidence? |
|----------|-------------------|
| Generic next steps | FORMAT — Could be fixed with "cheapest test to run this week" framing |
| n=20 feels thin | EVIDENCE — Needs larger samples or honest caveats |
| No behavioral validation | EVIDENCE — Stated preference ≠ revealed preference |
| Competitive landscape is shallow | FORMAT + EVIDENCE — AI-generated tables don't meet the bar |
| No segmentation in data | FORMAT — Data exists, report doesn't slice it |
| Quotes feel cherry-picked | FORMAT — Show contradicting quotes too |
| Pivot not validated | FORMAT — Apply same scrutiny to recommendations |

## Repeated Patterns Across Strong Reactions

What made personas say "this is genuinely useful":
- **Specific contradictions** — When data contradicted the founder's assumption, with quotes showing WHY
- **The uncomfortable truth** — Single most emotionally resonant section across all personas
- **Concrete numbers with implications** — "6/20 willing to teach, 16/20 won't wait 3 days" + "that's a marketplace death sentence"
- **Evidence that ChatGPT can't produce** — Real human quotes, real behavioral signals

## Repeated Patterns Across Weak Reactions

What made personas say "this feels weak":
- **Recommendations that aren't validated** — "Pivot to X" without testing X
- **Confidence exceeding evidence** — "CONFIRMED" / "REFUTED" on n=20 without caveats
- **AI-flavored analysis** — Competitive tables, risk matrices, economic theory that could be ChatGPT output
- **No methodology transparency** (except Variant B) — "Who are these 20 people?"
- **No bright spots section** — Every report tears down but doesn't help find what DOES work

## Which Format Should Advance to Real-Founder Testing?

**Variant B ("The Verdict") with targeted improvements.**

### Keep from Variant B:
- PROCEED / PIVOT / PAUSE top-line call
- Respondent transparency section
- Difficulty/risk ratings on next steps
- Confidence levels per assumption verdict

### Add from simulation feedback:
- **Evidence strength labels** per finding (e.g., "strong — backed by data + theory" / "directional — n=20" / "hypothesis — generated, not tested")
- **"Strongest signals" section** — What DID resonate in the data? Where's the bright spot?
- **"Cheapest next test" recommendation** — Not "pivot to X" but "here's what to test in 5 days to validate the pivot"
- **Show contradicting quotes** — Not just the ones that support the verdict
- **Explicit limitations disclaimer** at the top

### Drop (not worth adding):
- Full competitive landscape table (Variant C) — damaged credibility more than it helped. Consider a 2-sentence competitive note instead.
- Risk matrix (Variant C) — vibes, not analysis
- Business model math (Variant C) — only useful if sourced, which it can't be at this stage

## What Still Remains Uncertain

1. Whether real founders react the same as simulated personas (the core unknown)
2. Whether the PROCEED/PIVOT/PAUSE call feels too directive for some founders
3. Whether $49 is the right price (simulated personas have no wallets)
4. Whether the format works for ideas with less clear-cut findings (all test ideas had strong refutations — what about ambiguous results?)
5. Whether respondent transparency helps or just highlights how thin n=20 is

## What Should Be Logged in INTELLIGENCE.md

All [SIMULATED] Tier 4:

- **Hypothesis:** Assumption-verdict structure is the core format differentiator vs ChatGPT and generic surveys. Repeatedly cited across all personas and variants.
- **Hypothesis:** The "uncomfortable truth" section is the single highest-impact element. Every persona described it as the most emotionally resonant part.
- **Hypothesis:** Respondent transparency increases trust even when it reveals limitations (n=20, self-selected). Honesty about weakness builds credibility.
- **Hypothesis:** Top-line decision recommendation (PROCEED/PIVOT/PAUSE) increases actionability, especially for first-time founders who need the meta-decision made.
- **Hypothesis:** Competitive landscape tables and risk matrices from AI feel "fake-smart" and can damage credibility. Better omitted than poorly done.
- **Hypothesis:** Recommendations need the same scrutiny as the original idea. An unvalidated pivot suggestion is half a product.
- **Hypothesis:** "Cheapest next test" is more actionable than "strategic pivot recommendation." Founders want a 5-day experiment, not a new business plan.

2. Show it to 3-5 real founders (not simulated) — ideally founders who have recently gone through idea validation
